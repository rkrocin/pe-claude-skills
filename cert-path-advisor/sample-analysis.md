## Certification Recommendations

### Tier 1: High Impact / Close Now

#### AWS Solutions Architect Professional
**Why**: The JD says "AWS certifications preferred" and requires deep cloud architecture expertise. SA Associate is table stakes; Professional is the signal for Director-level architecture credibility. Your existing SA Associate knowledge transfers directly.
**Signal to hiring manager**: This candidate understands AWS at an enterprise architecture level, not just resource provisioning.
**Prerequisites**: SA Associate recommended (you have it)
**Preparation effort**: 6-8 weeks at 10 hrs/week (~70 hours)
**Cost**: $300 exam + ~$30 course = $330
**Difficulty**: Professional (challenging but achievable with your AWS experience)
**Overlap with current credentials**: SA Associate provides ~40% foundation, saving 2-3 weeks
**ROI assessment**: **High** - Directly addresses the biggest cloud credibility gap for a Director role

#### CISSP
**Why**: The JD requires partnering with the CISO on security strategy and PCI DSS compliance knowledge. CISSP is the gold standard for security leadership credibility in financial services. It signals that you can operate at a governance level, not just a technical level. It also partially closes the financial services domain gap.
**Signal to hiring manager**: This candidate can engage as a peer with the CISO and understands compliance at a governance level.
**Prerequisites**: 5 years experience in 2+ CISSP domains (you qualify based on infrastructure and cloud security experience)
**Preparation effort**: 8-10 weeks at 12 hrs/week (~100 hours)
**Cost**: $749 exam + ~$80 study materials = $830
**Difficulty**: Expert (significant commitment, but your infrastructure security background covers 3-4 domains)
**Overlap with current credentials**: Security+ covers ~15% of CISSP content at a surface level
**ROI assessment**: **High** - Near-universal requirement for security-adjacent leadership in FSI

---

### Tier 2: Strong Signal / Next Quarter

#### FinOps Certified Practitioner
**Why**: The JD requires $20M+ budget ownership. FinOps Practitioner signals structured cloud cost governance rather than ad-hoc cost management. Quick to obtain and immediately relevant.
**Signal to hiring manager**: This candidate approaches cloud spend as a discipline, not a side task.
**Prerequisites**: None
**Preparation effort**: 2-3 weeks at 8 hrs/week (~20 hours, training included with exam)
**Cost**: $300 (includes required training)
**Difficulty**: Foundation (relatively easy with your cloud background)
**Overlap with current credentials**: None directly, but your AWS experience covers the cloud concepts
**ROI assessment**: **High** - Low effort, directly addresses budget ownership signal

#### CCSP
**Why**: Bridges your CISSP knowledge (once obtained) with cloud security specifically. The JD requires cloud security at a governance level. CCSP is more cloud-specific than CISSP and signals depth in cloud security architecture.
**Signal to hiring manager**: This candidate understands cloud security at an architecture and governance level, not just configuration.
**Prerequisites**: 5 years IT experience (3 in security, 1 in cloud) - CISSP holders satisfy this
**Preparation effort**: 4-5 weeks at 10 hrs/week (~45 hours, significantly reduced if CISSP is obtained first due to 40% overlap)
**Cost**: $599 exam + ~$50 study materials = $650
**Difficulty**: Professional (manageable after CISSP)
**Overlap with current credentials**: 40% overlap with CISSP, saving 3-4 weeks
**ROI assessment**: **Medium-High** - Strong signal for cloud security in FSI, low incremental effort after CISSP

### Tier 3: Differentiator / This Year

#### AWS GenAI Developer Professional
**Why**: The JD mentions "AI-enabled developer experience." GenAI is the fastest-growing capability expectation for platform teams. This cert signals you can build with GenAI (RAG, agents, Bedrock), not just talk about it.
**Signal to hiring manager**: This candidate can lead the platform's AI enablement strategy with hands-on credibility.
**Prerequisites**: None
**Preparation effort**: 5-6 weeks at 10 hrs/week (~55 hours)
**Cost**: $300 exam + ~$30 course = $330
**Difficulty**: Professional (moderate, requires GenAI application concepts)
**Overlap with current credentials**: None directly, new domain
**ROI assessment**: **Medium** - Differentiator, not a gap-closer. High long-term value as AI becomes standard in platform roles.

#### CKA (Certified Kubernetes Administrator)
**Why**: Your resume mentions EKS experience but no Kubernetes certification. CKA validates operational Kubernetes expertise at a level that complements your Terraform Associate for infrastructure credibility.
**Signal to hiring manager**: This candidate can govern Kubernetes at scale, not just deploy to it.
**Prerequisites**: None (hands-on exam, your EKS experience is strong preparation)
**Preparation effort**: 5-7 weeks at 10 hrs/week (~60 hours)
**Cost**: $395 (includes one free retake)
**Difficulty**: Professional (performance-based, requires kubectl fluency)
**Overlap with current credentials**: Your EKS experience transfers directly
**ROI assessment**: **Medium** - Validates existing skills formally. Higher value if the target platform runs EKS.
